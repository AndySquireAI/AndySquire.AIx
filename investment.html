Strategic Partnership & Investment

Building the Governance Layer for Safe AI Support

The Context: A Responsible Path Forward

The global care crisis is real. Aging populations, caregiver shortages, and rising chronic disease burdens are placing unsustainable pressure on families, clinicians, and health systems. Technology has a role to play — but only if it is built with clinical integrity, human oversight, and patient safety at the center.

PatientCentricCare.AI is not pursuing speculative autonomous AI or consumer gadgets. We are building Safety OS — the governance and behavioral control layer that makes supervised AI assistance safe, predictable, and clinically acceptable for real-world home support.

We are advancing a responsible pathway toward AI-enabled support systems that maintain:

  • Clinical integrity
  • Human oversight
  • Patient safety
  • Transparent governance
  • Measurable outcomes

This presents a unique opportunity for strategic partners — not just capital investment, but co-creation of standards and evidence.

The Strategic Opportunity

PatientCentricCare.AI is not seeking speculative capital for unbounded AI exploration. We are building the Safety OS governance layer — a platform that enables supervised AI assistance in the home with:

  • Regulatory alignment
  • Risk mitigation
  • Protocol-based behavior
  • Clinician integration
  • Scalability across care settings

This foundation unlocks multiple strategic use cases, including chronic care support and post-discharge continuity.

Sponsors and investors who engage early have the chance to shape: Clinical evidence protocols, Safety OS governance models, policy and reimbursement frameworks, and longitudinal data strategies.

What We Are Building: Safety OS — The Governing Layer

Safety OS is a regulatory-grade behavioral control framework that ensures every AI-supported action is predictable, supervised, and audit-ready.

It includes:

  • Human-in-the-loop oversight: Clinicians remain in control of all critical decisions
  • Protocol and boundary enforcement: Every action is mapped to defined clinical protocols
  • Risk detection and fail-safe escalation: Real-time monitoring with safe transitions
  • Transparent logging and audit trails: Complete documentation for review and compliance
  • Privacy-first data handling: HIPAA-aligned with explicit consent

Evidence-First Pathway
