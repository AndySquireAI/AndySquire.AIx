How Safety OS Works

Click each layer to learn more.

Layer 1: Applications

Home Companion — the user-facing product

Home Companion is the first deployment of Safety OS. It provides conversation, medication reminders, cognitive engagement, and voice communication for seniors aging at home. It is the interface — not the product. The product is the governance underneath.
↓

Layer 2: Safety OS

The governance kernel — authority enforcement at runtime

Safety OS enforces authority boundaries, escalation logic, consent constraints, and immutable audit logging for every AI interaction. It sits below the application layer and above the AI engines. No AI action executes without passing through Safety OS.
↓

Layer 3: AI Engines

LLMs and predictive models — constrained by governance

The AI layer handles natural language processing, intent recognition, and predictive analytics. But every output is filtered through Safety OS before reaching the user. The AI never acts autonomously — it operates within boundaries defined by clinicians and caregivers.

How AI Earns Permission to Act in Healthcare

Phase I — Non-Medical AI

Human-in-the-loop systems with no clinical inference. Companionship, reminders, and structured engagement only.

Phase II — SaMD-Ready, Physician Supervised

Escalation-only execution within clinician-defined boundaries. Physician-as-Pilot governance model.

Phase III — Regulated Clinical AI (SaMD)

Licensed clinical authority with controlled inference. Full regulatory compliance and audit trail.

Invariant: Clinical authority is never delegated to AI.
