AI in the Home Is Scaling Faster Than Governance

AI systems are entering private living environments without authority constraints. What is missing:

No clear authority boundaries

Most home AI systems operate without defined limits on what the AI can and cannot do in sensitive contexts.

No explicit consent enforcement

Escalation and data sharing happen without structured user consent or governance-gated pathways.

No deterministic escalation logic

When something goes wrong, there is no structured, auditable handoff to human authority.

No immutable audit logging

Without timestamped, boundary-classified records, there is no defensible deployment posture and no regulatory inheritance.

Retrofitting governance after scale is expensive, legally risky, and often impossible without architectural redesign.

Phase I: Home Companion

A deliberately non-medical deployment environment used to validate governance maturity before clinical expansion.

  • Structured conversational interaction within governance boundaries
  • Scheduled interaction cycles with boundary enforcement
  • Human-confirmed escalation only
  • No diagnosis, no medical advice
  • Designed explicitly outside clinical claims

Phase I hardware: familiar form factor with embedded governance kernel

Tier 0 Acoustic Privacy

  • Edge-level processing captures non-semantic interaction metadata (e.g., noise level, cadence)
  • Raw audio is not persistently stored
  • Audit logs capture event classification — not private speech content

The Safety OS "Flight Recorder"

Governance is enforced at runtime. Not documented in a PDF. Enforced in the system.

  • Every interaction is time-stamped
  • Every boundary event is classified
  • Every escalation is consent-logged
  • Every record is reviewable post-incident

This is not a policy document.It is an operational control plane.

Structured Audit Output

Every interaction generates structured, time-stamped records including session ID, escalation triggers, boundary refusals, consent confirmations, and event classification.

Designed to Scale — Only When Earned
