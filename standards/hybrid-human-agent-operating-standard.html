Safety OS™ — A Human-Agent Teaming Control Architecture

Version 2.0

Safety OS™ is a Human-Agent Teaming (HAT) Control Architecture and Authority Standard designed to enable scalable, accountable AI deployment in regulated and high-stakes environments.

Safety OS implements a governance-first control plane that separates authority, enforces bounded autonomy, and makes accountability inspectable — not by accident, but by design.

Unlike generic AI governance frameworks, Safety OS operationalizes authority partitioning, deterministic escalation, consent enforcement, and immutable auditability — enabling AI augmentation to scale safely without surrendering human accountability.

Executive Summary

The Problem Today

AI deployment increases speed and scale but leaves accountability ambiguous. Existing governance relies on committees and retrospective review — which fail in real-time, safety-critical contexts.

Safety OS Solves This By

  • Explicitly separating authority from execution
  • Encoding governance invariants into the control plane
  • Ensuring humans retain intent, risk, and escalation ownership
  • Machines execute bounded autonomy with runtime enforcement
  • Embodied agents (voice agents, robots, apps) become extensions of the Safety OS control layer

Safety OS Enables

  • Scalable AI adoption that survives regulatory scrutiny
  • Clear ownership before incidents occur
  • Traceable, real-time governance at machine speed
  • Auditability and compliance readiness

Core Architectural Principles

Safety OS is grounded in five enforceable invariants:

1 Explicit Authority Partitioning

Humans retain binding authority; agents execute within defined permissions.

2 Bounded Autonomy

Every capability must have an assigned autonomy level. Unclassified capabilities cannot execute.

3 Deterministic Escalation

Escalation logic is pre-defined, auditable, and irreversible until resolved.

4 Consent Lifecycle Enforcement

Active consent is tracked, enforced, and timestamped for every interaction.

5 Immutable Auditability

All interactions, decisions, and escalations are logged in an audit-ready form.

Control Plane vs. Embodied Agents

Safety OS is a control plane — not an interface.

Embodiments (voice agent, app UI, humanoid robot, wearable) are execution nodes that rely on the control plane for authority decisions, escalation gating, and audit propagation.

The Safety OS Control Plane is the source of truth for:

  • Identity & consent context
  • Role-based capability activation
  • Escalation gating policies
  • Runtime enforcement
  • Audit trace generation

Agents are execution enforcers — the Control Plane is the governance engine.

Illustrative governance architecture: maps authority, enforcement, and escalation across deployment contexts. Does not imply commercial readiness, autonomous clinical action, or regulatory approval.

Three Governance Layers

1. Public Doctrine Layer

Core governance principles, rationale, and definitions. Published openly for citation, research, and standard-building.

2. Reference Architecture Layer

Safe, high-level governance patterns and role maps. Published as narrative and diagrams for implementation guidance.

3. Safety OS Core (Proprietary)

Runtime enforcement engine, deterministic logic, escalation mechanisms, and audit engine. This is the executable control plane — the defendable IP.

The Four Safety OS Artifacts

1. Decision Inventory

What decisions exist and who owns them.

2. Autonomy Allocation Matrix

Which agent or human executes each decision.

3. Accountability & Escalation Framework

Defines chains of authority, rollback conditions, and fatigue-resilient gating.

4. Governance as Infrastructure

Encoded, runtime policies, trap-doors, kill switches, non-ambiguous interventions.

Safety OS Progression Model

Safety OS supports staged adoption for regulated deployment:

Phase I — Caregiver-in-the-Loop

  • Non-clinical workflows
  • Caregiver escalation validated
  • Identity, consent, escalation gating tested

Phase II — Physician-as-Pilot

  • Clinical workflows
  • Humans retain final authority
  • Agent execution scoped and reversible

Phase III — AIaMD / SaMD-Aligned

  • High-risk clinical modules
  • MDR / FDA / EU AI Act alignment
  • Physician authority remains upstream
  • Safety OS ensures regulatory continuity
