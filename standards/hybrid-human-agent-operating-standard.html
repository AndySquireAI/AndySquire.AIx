Hybrid Human–Agent Operating Standard

Version 1.0

A vendor-neutral operating standard for allocating decision authority between humans and autonomous AI systems.

Why this Standard exists

As autonomous AI systems become embedded in high-stakes environments—healthcare, finance, infrastructure, and beyond—organizations face a critical governance gap. Existing frameworks address either human decision-making or AI capabilities in isolation, but provide no structured method for allocating authority between them.

This Standard exists to close that gap: to provide a rigorous, auditable, and vendor-neutral framework for defining where human authority ends and agent autonomy begins—and how accountability flows across that boundary.

What this Standard addresses

The Hybrid Human–Agent Operating Standard addresses the operational and governance challenges that arise when humans and AI agents share decision-making authority. Specifically, it provides:

  • A structured method for inventorying decisions within a system or workflow
  • A framework for allocating autonomy levels to each decision type
  • Clear accountability and escalation pathways when boundaries are crossed
  • Governance infrastructure requirements for auditability and compliance

What this Standard is

This is an operating standard—a set of principles, artifacts, and governance structures designed to be adopted, adapted, and implemented by organizations deploying human-agent systems. It is:

  • Vendor-neutral: Applicable to any AI system, platform, or provider
  • Domain-agnostic: Core principles apply across healthcare, finance, operations, and other sectors
  • Auditable: Designed to produce documentation suitable for regulatory review
  • Open: Published under a permissive license for broad adoption

What this Standard is not

This Standard does not:

  • Prescribe specific AI technologies, models, or vendors
  • Replace existing regulatory frameworks (e.g., FDA, EU AI Act, HIPAA)
  • Provide legal advice or guarantee regulatory compliance
  • Define technical implementation details or software architecture

Organizations remain responsible for ensuring their implementations comply with applicable laws and regulations in their jurisdictions.

Core principles

  • Human authority is explicit: Authority must be consciously allocated, not assumed or inherited
  • Autonomy is bounded: Agent autonomy operates within defined constraints, not open-ended permissions
  • Accountability is traceable: Every decision must have a clear accountability chain
  • Escalation is deterministic: Boundary conditions trigger predictable escalation pathways
  • Governance is infrastructure: Oversight mechanisms are built into system architecture, not bolted on

Who this Standard is for

This Standard is designed for:

  • AI Governance Leaders: Establishing organizational policies for human-agent systems
  • Product and Engineering Teams: Building systems with governance requirements embedded
  • Compliance and Risk Officers: Documenting decision authority for regulatory review
  • Healthcare, Finance, and Critical Infrastructure Operators: Deploying AI in high-stakes environments
  • Regulators and Auditors: Evaluating organizational AI governance practices

Neutrality & stewardship

This Standard is published and maintained by PatientCentricCare.AI as a public good. It is not tied to any specific product, platform, or commercial offering. The Standard's development is guided by principles of transparency, broad applicability, and continuous improvement based on implementation feedback.

Organizations are encouraged to contribute feedback, case studies, and sector-specific adaptations to strengthen the Standard over time.
