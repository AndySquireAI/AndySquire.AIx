Version 1.0

An operator-first AI-in-the-Loop (AIITL) governance model

The problem this standard solves

Organizations introducing AI into existing processes face a predictable failure mode:

  • AI increases speed and scale
  • Accountability remains ambiguous
  • Oversight relies on committees and hope
  • When something goes wrong, escalation is slow and blame is retroactive

This is an operating-model problem, not a tooling problem.

AIITL: humans retain ownership of intent, risk, and escalation. Machines execute, monitor, and enforce controls at runtime.

Before vs After: What actually changes

Medical information inquiry handling. Same pattern applies to safety signal triage, claims routing, credit decisions, or content moderation.

Before: human-only model

  • Manual triage, research, draft, review, approve
  • Controls rely on SOPs and peer review
  • Throughput constrained by human capacity
  • Oversight happens after execution
  • Turnaround: 2‚Äì3 days

After: hybrid human‚Äìagent model

  • AI performs triage, retrieval, synthesis, drafting
  • Each decision step has explicit autonomy level
  • Guardrails, sampling, alerts operate continuously
  • Humans review exceptions and escalations only
  • Turnaround: hours, with stronger traceability

The core shift

Most AI governance asks: "How autonomous is this system?"

This standard asks: "Which specific decisions exist, who owns them, and how much autonomy is allowed for each?"

  • Every decision is explicitly inventoried
  • Assigned a single accountable human owner
  • Given a defined autonomy level (0‚Äì4)
  • Monitored for drift, incidents, and policy changes
  • Able to regress instantly when conditions change

If a decision is not inventoried, owned, and classified, it is not eligible for AI autonomy.

The Four Artifacts

Implemented as a loop, not standalone documents.

1. Decision Inventory

Defines what decisions exist and who owns their outcomes.

  • Decisions classified by impact, frequency, reversibility, risk
  • Each decision has a single accountable human owner
  • Uninventoried decisions cannot be automated

2. Autonomy Allocation Matrix

Defines who (human or AI) executes each decision, and when.

  • Autonomy assigned per decision, not per system
  • Five levels from human-only to high-scale AI execution
  • Higher autonomy requires stronger evidence and controls
  • Regression must be as easy as advancement

3. Accountability & Escalation Framework

Defines who absorbs downside when something goes wrong.

  • Single accountability chain per decision
  • Incidents classified before blame
  • Explicit rollback authority defined in advance
  • Learning feeds back into future autonomy decisions

4. Governance as Infrastructure

Defines how rules are enforced at machine speed.

  • Policies encoded in machine-compatible form
  • Decisions observable, auditable, traceable
  • Intervention and rollback automated where possible

If governance cannot be executed by machines, it will be bypassed by machines.

HITL / HOTL compatibility

AIITL makes existing Human-in-the-Loop and Human-on-the-Loop models operational by anchoring them to specific decisions, binding them to explicit accountability, and enforcing them through infrastructure.

What this enables

  • Faster execution without surrendering accountability
  • Scalable AI adoption that survives regulatory scrutiny
  • Clear ownership before incidents occur
  • Governance that works at AI speed

Safety OS at a Glance

  • Governance-first execution model for AI agents
  • Enforces consent, authority, escalation, and auditability at runtime
  • Enables safe progression from non-medical AI to regulated clinical systems

Core principles

  • Human authority is explicit: Authority must be consciously allocated, not assumed
  • Autonomy is bounded: Agent autonomy operates within defined constraints
  • Accountability is traceable: Every decision has a clear accountability chain
  • Escalation is deterministic: Boundary conditions trigger predictable pathways
  • Governance is infrastructure: Oversight built into system architecture
