# Curated: Wearable Health Tech in 2025: What's Worth Your Money?

Published: October 11, 2025
Category: Patient Resources
Reading Time: 6 minutes

Introduction

The wearable technology market is flooded with devices promising to track our health, from smartwatches and rings to continuous glucose monitors and smart patches. But which ones are genuinely useful medical tools, and which are just expensive gadgets? We've curated the latest insights from experts at The Verge and Wirecutter to help you decide.

1. Smartwatches: The All-in-One Health Hub

  • Top Contenders: Apple Watch Series 10, Google Pixel Watch 3, Samsung Galaxy Watch 7.
  • Key Features: ECG, blood oxygen monitoring, heart rate variability (HRV), sleep tracking, and fall detection.
  • Expert Take: Smartwatches are becoming powerful health monitoring tools. The ECG and irregular heart rhythm notifications are FDA-cleared and can provide early warnings for conditions like atrial fibrillation. While not a replacement for medical-grade devices, they are excellent for establishing a baseline and tracking trends.
  • Worth it? Yes, for individuals who want a comprehensive overview of their daily health and fitness.
  • Source: The Verge

2. Smart Rings: For a Good Night's Sleep

  • Top Contenders: Oura Ring 4, Ultrahuman Ring Air.
  • Key Features: Advanced sleep tracking (deep, REM, light sleep), body temperature, and activity monitoring.
  • Expert Take: Smart rings excel at sleep analysis. Their minimal design makes them more comfortable to wear overnight than a watch. They provide detailed insights into sleep quality and recovery, which are crucial for overall health.
  • Worth it? Yes, for those who want to optimize their sleep and recovery without the distractions of a smartwatch screen.
  • Source: Wirecutter

3. Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs): Not Just for Diabetics

  • Top Contenders: Dexcom G7, Abbott FreeStyle Libre 3.
  • Key Features: Real-time blood sugar monitoring without fingerpricks.
  • Expert Take: Originally designed for diabetics, CGMs are now being used by health-conscious individuals to understand how diet and exercise impact their metabolic health. Seeing the immediate effect of food on blood sugar can be a powerful motivator for healthier eating habits.
  • Worth it? For diabetics, it's a must-have. For others, it can be a valuable tool for personalized nutrition, but it's a significant ongoing expense.
  • Source: Healthline

4. Smart Patches: The Future of Remote Monitoring

  • Top Contenders: Philips Biosensor, VitalConnect VitalPatch.
  • Key Features: Continuous monitoring of vital signs like heart rate, respiratory rate, and skin temperature for up to 14 days.
  • Expert Take: These medical-grade patches are primarily used in hospitals for remote patient monitoring, but they are starting to become available for at-home use. They provide a level of data that was previously only available in a clinical setting, enabling early detection of health issues.
  • Worth it? For patients with chronic conditions or those recovering from surgery, these can be life-savers. For the average person, they are not yet necessary.
  • Source: MobiHealthNews

The Bottom Line

When choosing a wearable health device, consider your specific goals. For general wellness, a smartwatch or smart ring is a great start. For managing a specific condition, a medical-grade device like a CGM or smart patch may be more appropriate. Always consult with your doctor before making any significant changes to your health routine based on data from a wearable device.
