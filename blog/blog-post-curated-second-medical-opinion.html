# Curated: How to Get a Second Medical Opinion (And When You Should)

Published: October 11, 2025
Category: Patient Resources
Reading Time: 4 minutes

Introduction

Navigating a serious medical diagnosis can be overwhelming. One of the most empowering steps you can take is to seek a second opinion. This curated guide, with insights from the American Cancer Society and Cleveland Clinic, explains when and how to get one.

When to Seek a Second Opinion

According to the experts, you should consider getting a second opinion if:

  • You have been diagnosed with a rare or life-threatening condition.
  • The recommended treatment is risky, involves surgery, or has lifelong consequences.
  • Your diagnosis is unclear, or you have multiple medical problems.
  • You feel your doctor is not listening to you or is dismissing your concerns.

Source: American Cancer Society

How to Get a Second Opinion

  1. Talk to Your Current Doctor: Most doctors are supportive of patients seeking a second opinion. Ask for a copy of your medical records and a referral to another specialist.

  2. Check with Your Insurance: Most insurance plans will cover a second opinion, but it's always a good idea to check your policy first.

  3. Find a Specialist: Look for a doctor who has experience treating your specific condition. You can ask your current doctor for a recommendation, or search online databases from organizations like the American Medical Association.

  4. Schedule an Appointment: When you schedule the appointment, let the office know that you are seeking a second opinion. This will help them to prepare for your visit.

  5. Prepare for Your Appointment: Make a list of your questions and bring a copy of your medical records, including any test results or imaging scans.

Source: Cleveland Clinic

What to Do with a Second Opinion

If the second opinion confirms your original diagnosis and treatment plan, you can feel more confident moving forward. If the opinions differ, you may need to weigh the pros and cons of each approach or even seek a third opinion.

Ultimately, the decision is yours. A second opinion is a valuable tool to help you make the most informed choice about your health.

Remember, you are your own best advocate. Don't be afraid to ask questions, seek out information, and take an active role in your healthcare journey.
